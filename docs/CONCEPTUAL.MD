Managing SEO During a Domain Change
Losing SEO is a valid concern, but it's entirely manageable. Since your current SEO isn't a massive asset, the migration process is much lower risk. Here’s how you turn this into a major win:

The "Change of Address" - 301 Redirects:
As you mentioned, redirects are key. You will implement "301 Redirects" from every page on solampio.com to its new equivalent on your new domain (e.g., solampliving.com). A 301 redirect permanently tells search engines like Google, "We have moved forever. Please transfer all of our authority, rankings, and 'link juice' to this new address." It's the official, correct way to do it.

Tell Google Directly:
Inside Google Search Console (a free tool you should be using), there is a specific "Change of Address" tool. This allows you to explicitly tell Google that your site has moved from the old domain to the new one. This dramatically speeds up the process of Google recognizing the change and transferring your SEO value.

The "Fresh Start" Opportunity:
This is the most exciting part. You're not just moving a site; you're launching a better one.

Performance: Your new Qwik site will likely be much faster and more modern than the old one. Google loves fast websites. This is an immediate SEO boost.

Clear Structure: We're designing a site with a clearer structure (D2C vs. B2B, a dedicated Learning Hub). This makes it easier for Google to understand what you're about.

Content is King: Your plan to leverage Christopher LaForge for content is an SEO goldmine. Authoritative, expert-written content is exactly what Google wants to rank. You can build a much stronger SEO foundation on this new site than you ever could have on the old one.

In short, you're trading a little short-term turbulence for a massive long-term gain in both brand clarity and SEO potential.
